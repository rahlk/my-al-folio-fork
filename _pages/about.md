I'm a Senior Research Scientist at [IBM Research](https://research.ibm.com/teams/application-modernization), where my work sits at the intersection of *large language models* and *program analysis* for software engineering. My current research builds agentic systems that bridge code and IT operations — spanning root-cause analysis of cloud incidents ([*Praxis*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.22113)), automated test generation ([*ASTER*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.03093) and [*SAINT*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.13305)), code translation and modernization ([*SpecTra*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.18574), [*Lost in Translation*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.03109), and [*C2SaferRust*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.14257)), and the open-source [CodeLLM-DevKit (CLDK)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.13007) framework, a cross-language middleware layer that grounds agents with program-analysis context.

Earlier work spans AI-guided microservice migration ([*CARGO*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2207.11784)), crowdtesting management ([*iSENSE*](https://2019.icse-conferences.org/details/icse-2019-Technical-Papers/32/iSENSE-Completion-Aware-Crowdtesting-Management)), and deep-learning vulnerability detection. My research appears regularly at the top venues in software engineering and systems (ICSE, FSE, ASE, OOPSLA, EuroSys, TSE, DSN).

My broader interests include software security (particularly vulnerability detection and fuzzing) and causal inference (applied to diagnosing and repairing performance faults and reasoning about configurable-system behavior) — themes from my postdoc at Columbia University. Earlier still, I completed a Ph.D. at NC State University on actionable analytics and transfer learning for software engineering.
